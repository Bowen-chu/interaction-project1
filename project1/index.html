Jorges Luis Borges

In a dream as quiet as velvet, I find myself reading a leather-bound book. The protagonist of the book is also dreaming. In his dream, he is trapped inside a labyrinth. No matter which path he takes, every turn leads him back to the beginning. The repetition persists, and his anxiety steadily grows. When panic reaches its peak, he chooses a form of escape that seems almost contradictory: he stops moving. He remains where he is, allows himself to relax, and wakes up from the reality of the book. At the same moment, I wake up in my own room. After waking, I open the book beside me and read its final paragraph. The character lifts his head from the desk and realizes—as I do—that he has finally escaped the labyrinth.
